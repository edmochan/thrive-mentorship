# Giving and Receiving Professional Feedback
Feedback is an incredibly important part of professional growth and development. Feedback can help you to enhance your skills, clarify expectations, improve communication with your colleagues and peers, and become more confident in your work. Feedback is a normal and expected part of a professional environment. Giving constructive feedback and receiving feedback respectfully are core skills for working in any industry. This guide will focus on understanding effective feedback and learning the best ways to give and receive it.

## 1\. Giving Effective Feedback

Good professional feedback is clear, kind, and actionable. Effective feedback to a peer, colleague, or other professional contact should always:

#### 1\. Be specific and actionable

- Focus on clear, concrete observations to help the recipient understand the scope of the feedback.
- Provide guidance on what to do next to make any necessary improvements.
- The more specific you can be with your comments, the more likely the recipient can act on them appropriately.

#### 2\. Be balanced but respectful

- Include both strengths and areas for improvement. Feedback that is exclusively positive or exclusively negative is less useful.
- Wording should focus on the work product and not the person.
- Remember that the goal of feedback is to help someone improve their work. Comments should always remain professional and not personal.

#### 3\. Be timely

- Provide feedback close to when the work is done.
- Feedback does not need to be immediate, but should be provided close to the time of the work for greatest impact.

## 2\. Structuring Professional Feedback

If you struggle to word your feedback in a way that is direct but still respectful, consider following this structure:

- **Observation:** What do you see?
- **Impact:** Why does that matter?
- **Suggestion:** What could they try next?

Avoid being overly critical within your feedback. Feedback that is overly negative will not be well received and thus will not be useful. You should also limit the amount of feedback you provide at one time. This can overwhelm the recipient and limit their ability to respond appropriately. Provide specific and actionable insights and use an iterative dialogue to continue the conversation as needed.

### Feedback prompts

- "One strength I see is … "
- "One area to improve is …"
- "You might consider …"
- "One suggestion would be …"

### Example

- **Observation:** "Your project statement is clear and concise."
- **Impact:** "Adding a bullet point with a measurable result would make it stronger for employers."
- **Suggestion:** "You could include the number of users or the improvement it achieved to demonstrate the success of the project."

## 3\. Receiving Professional Feedback

It can be uncomfortable to receive feedback, and that is normal! It can be difficult to separate your personal feelings from your professional work. Feedback is a normal and necessary part of your professional life. Receiving feedback in a respectful and mature manner is a critical part of being a good colleague and employee. If you struggle with receiving constructive criticism, try the steps below.

- **Listen.** Do not interrupt the person providing the feedback. Focus on understanding their point rather than explaining or defending yourself.
- **Ask** clarifying questions as needed. ("Can you show me an example of what you mean?")
- **Reflect** before reacting. Not all feedback requires immediate action. Think about what they said and how you can apply the feedback moving forward.
- **Apply** the feedback where appropriate.
- **Follow up**. Ask for additional feedback if needed (possibly from another person).

Think of feedback as a continuous loop in your professional life. You do work, receive feedback, reflect on it, apply changes, and repeat. Growth happens through this iteration. You do not need to be embarrassed when you are not perfect!

## 4\. Feedback in the Mentor-Mentee Relationship

Mentors should expect to give feedback to mentees in a number of ways, including resume reviews, mock interviews, communication practice, and weekly reflections. Students should ask for feedback on any of these topics (or others!) along the way. Take notes on the feedback and how you applied it to reflect on your mentorship relationship.

### Guidance for Mentors

- Prioritize the most important feedback
- Connect the feedback to the student's goals where possible
- Reinforce the student's progress towards their goals and professional growth
- Balance between supportive words and a challenge to grow
- Ask the student to reflect on the feedback, and accept their comments. Remember, feedback is a loop!

### Guidance for Mentees

- Always reflect on the feedback, even if you do not agree with it initially. Not all feedback is perfect! But it is worth investigating whether there is something to learn from it.
- Think about changes you could make to address the feedback.
- Ask questions if things seem unclear. Follow the Professional Communication Guide if you need help forming these questions.

## 5\. Common Challenges in Professional Feedback

- "I don't know what to say."
  - Try the 3-step structure above (Observation → Impact → Suggestion)
  - Engage in an open dialogue and clarify any points as needed
- "This feedback feels critical."
  - Remember, feedback is meant to help you improve!
  - Receiving criticism is a normal part of work and does not reflect on you personally. Be open to the idea of ways you can improve as a colleague and employee.
- "This was too much feedback at once."
  - For mentors: focus on 2 to 3 key points rather than many details. Balance supportiveness with growth.
  - For mentees: reflect on the feedback and see if you can find a few overarching themes within the feedback. Ask for guidance as needed.
- "I don't agree with this feedback."
  - Think about whether you can learn something from the notes, even if you do not agree with every word.
  - Are there parts you can apply?
  - Seek feedback from another trusted mentor or colleague. Sometimes another opinion can help solidify what you need to do and how you can apply it.
