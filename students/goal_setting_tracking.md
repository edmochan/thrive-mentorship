# Student Goal Setting and Tracking Guide
The purpose of this tool is to help you set clear and meaningful goals, track your progress towards those goals, and remain accountable in achieving the goals. Each goal includes:

- SMART Goal Statement
- Step-by-step plan to reach milestones
- If-Then Plan to anticipate obstacles
- Weekly Progress Tracking
- Reflection & Adjustment

Use the Goal Tracker to help you keep on top of the goals you set. This document explains the purpose and utility of each section of the tracker.

## 1\. SMART Goals and Goal Setting

SMART is an acronym for a type of goal that is well-defined and meaningful to you. SMART goals are:

- **Specific:** What exactly do you want to accomplish?
- **Measurable:** How will you know you succeeded?
- **Achievable:** Is this realistic right now?
- **Relevant:** Why does this matter for your goals?
- **Time-bound:** When will you complete it?

Using the SMART format helps ensure that the goals you set are specific, actionable, and manageable. Vague goals are hard to stick to. SMART goals add sufficient detail to help you stay on track.

Each goal you enter in the goal tracker should be in the SMART format to make it as specific as possible. For example, "I want to get an internship" is not a specific enough goal. Rephrase it as "I will apply to 15 software engineering internships by November 1 by submitting 3 applications per week through the month of October.

### SMART Goal templates

**Goal Title:**  
**Type:** ☐ Academic ☐ Career ☐ Skill Development

**SMART Goal**

I will __**\[goal\]**__ by __**\[date\]**__ by doing __**\[action\]**__.

## 2\. Milestone Planning

Setting goals is important, but reaching them can feel overwhelming. Breaking goals down into multiple, manageable steps with short-term deadlines can help you stay on track. Think about what steps would be required to achieve your goal, and in what order you need to complete those steps.

### Milestone tracker template example

| **Milestone**             | **Target Date** | **Status** | **Notes** |
| ------------------------- | --------------- | ---------- | --------- |
| Resume updated            | Sept 15         | ☐          |           |
| 3 applications submitted  | Oct 3           | ☐          |           |
| 6 applications submitted  | Oct 10          | ☐          |           |
| 9 applications submitted  | Oct 17          | ☐          |           |
| 12 applications submitted | Oct 24          | ☐          |           |
| 15 applications submitted | Oct 31          | ☐          |           |

## 3\. "If-Then" Planning for Obstacles

Sometimes obstacles can block your path to success in your goals. Problems with time management, uncertainty, and motivation can hinder your progress towards your goals. Use the If-Then template in the Goal Tracker to identify obstacles you may encounter and how you plan to handle them.

### If-Then tracker template examples

| **If (Obstacle)**            | **Then (Action Plan)**                         |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| If I feel overwhelmed        | Then I will break the task into smaller steps  |
| If I don't know how to start | Then I will ask my mentor or look for examples |
| If I miss a week             | Then I will adjust my plan and continue        |

---

## 4\. Weekly Progress Tracker

Spend a few minutes at the end of each week to look back on your progress. List the action you took that week (based on the steps you outlined previously) and estimate your progress towards that goal. Reflect on any challenges you met in completing the action, and decide on your next step to take the following week. Be honest with yourself! It is okay if you did not meet a goal in a given week. Acknowledge your challenges and decide how to improve things the following week.

### Weekly progress tracker template examples

| **Week** | **Action Taken**   | **Progress (%)** | **Challenges**            | **Next Step**             |
| -------- | ------------------ | ---------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Week 1   | Updated resume     | 20%              | Not sure about formatting | Get feedback              |
| Week 2   | Applied to 3 roles | 40%              | Time constraints          | Schedule application time |

---

## 5\. Reflection and Adjustment

You should take time during your progress tracking to reflect on your goals and your ability to achieve them. Below are some guiding questions for your reflections.

### Midpoint reflection

- Am I making progress?
- Are my milestones realistic?
- What needs to change?

### Final reflection

- Did I achieve my goal? Why or why not?
- What did I learn?
- What is my next goal?

## 6\. Mentor Integration in Goal-Setting

Mentors should engage with the goal-setting process by reviewing the students' goals, checking in on their progress at meetings, and helping to refine unrealistic goals or deadlines. Discuss the goals and determine if they are specific and actionable. Ask the students how they plan to approach each goal, and help them see what challenges they might face in reaching these goals. You may also help the students come up with alternatives or backups as needed. Adapting to change and overcoming failures is a major part of the hidden curriculum that students often do not experience much of prior to college.
