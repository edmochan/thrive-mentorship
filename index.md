# THRIVE
## Toolkit of Holistic Resources for Intentional and Versatile Engagement

*A mentoring toolkit for undergraduate computer science students and their industry mentors*

---

## What Makes THRIVE Different

- **Proactive, not reactive.** Giving students access to a mentor before they know they need help.
  THRIVE prioritizes building professional identity
  and community early, not after disengagement has already begun.

- **Student-led.** Students initiate and shape their mentoring relationships, 
  developing the self-advocacy and professional communication skills required for successful future careers.

- **Both sides of the relationship.** Most mentoring resources address 
  only students or only mentors. THRIVE provides structured support for both groups simultaneously, creating a shared
  vocabulary for mentors and mentees to aid communication and make meetings more effective.
  
- **Grounded in research.** THRIVE is built on established mentoring 
  frameworks and four years of outcome data showing meaningful differences 
  in GPA, retention, and graduation rates between program participants 
  and non-participants.

- **Freely available and adaptable.** While THRIVE was originally developed for a small and new computer science major program, it is designed for any 
  STEM program with access to industry professionals, regardless of 
  institution size, budget, or industry partner.

## Program Outcomes

Over four years at Carlow University, 40 undergraduate CS students 
were invited to participate in the THRIVE mentoring program.

| Outcome | Participants (n=20) | Non-Participants (n=20) |
|---|---|---|
| Mean GPA | 3.52 (SD = 0.44) | 2.32 (SD = 1.62) |
| Retained in CS / Graduated | 17 (85%) | 9 (45%) |
| Dismissed / Transferred | 3 (15%) | 11 (55%) |

*Participation in mentorship is associated with higher retention, 
greater participation in activities outside the classroom, and more 
stable academic outcomes.*
